The Problem

A community full of
potential, held back
by access barriers

  • 📵
    No local digital infrastructure
    Residents must leave the community for internet access, printing, and essential government services — costing time and money they don't have.
  • 📉
    High youth unemployment
    Without digital skills and formal training pathways, young people in Kibagare are locked out of the modern economy before they start.
  • 📖
    Educational disadvantage
    Children lack academic support and digital learning resources, compounding generational inequality from an early age.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧
    Overwhelmed families
    Many parents and guardians struggle to balance work and household responsibilities. The Launchpad now offers affordable school uniform laundry services to support families while children participate in learning activities.
  • 🚀
    The Launchpad is the solution
    Providing digital empowerment, educational support, and practical family services for the Kibagare community — all in one place, built for the long term.

How it works

A self-sustaining model built
for long-term impact

Revenue from digital services, training, printing, and laundry directly funds community programs — creating a virtuous cycle of empowerment and sustainability.

🎓
LEARN
Community members access digital skills training, homework support, and educational resources
💻
WORK
Trained youth gain employment at the Launchpad or find work in the wider digital economy
💰
EARN
Digital services, printing, laundry, and training fees create a financially sustainable operation
🤝
MENTOR
Empowered community members give back — mentoring the next generation and multiplying impact
🔄 Sustainability by design
Multiple revenue streams — digital services, printing, eCitizen support, training fees, and laundry services — ensure the Launchpad is not dependent on any single income source or grant cycle.
👕 Laundry services revenue
School uniform laundry is both a practical community service and a steady revenue stream — generating income while directly supporting families and keeping children in school clean and dignified.
🏘️ Owned by the community
All five roles are filled from within Kibagare. Value stays local. Skills are built locally. The centre grows the capacity of its own people — not outside consultants.
🌍 Designed to scale
The model is replicable. Once proven in Kibagare, this framework — digital services, education support, and practical family services — can be adapted across other underserved communities in Kenya and beyond.
