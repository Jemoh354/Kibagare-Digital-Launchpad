The Problem

A community full of
potential, held back
by access barriers

  • 📵
    No local digital infrastructure
    Residents must leave the community for internet access, printing, and essential government services — costing time and money they don't have.
  • 📉
    High youth unemployment
    Without digital skills and formal training pathways, young people in Kibagare are locked out of the modern economy before they start.
  • 📖
    Educational disadvantage
    Children lack academic support and digital learning resources, compounding generational inequality from an early age.
  • 🚀
    The Launchpad is the solution
    One centre. Multiple services. Long-term community self-reliance. The Kibagare Digital Launchpad addresses each of these barriers — together.

How it works

A self-sustaining model built
for long-term impact

Revenue from services directly funds community programs — creating a virtuous cycle of empowerment and sustainability.

🎓
LEARN
Community members access digital skills training and educational support at the centre
💻
WORK
Trained youth gain employment at the Launchpad or find work in the wider digital economy
💰
EARN
Revenue-generating services create a financially sustainable operation that doesn't depend on donations
🤝
MENTOR
Empowered community members give back — mentoring the next generation and multiplying impact
🔄 Sustainability by design
Multiple revenue streams — digital services, printing, repairs, training fees, and gaming — ensure the Launchpad is not dependent on any single income source or grant cycle.
📈 Profit that serves people
Surplus revenue is reinvested into free community programs, subsidised services for those who cannot pay, and the expansion of the centre's capacity and reach.
🏘️ Owned by the community
All five roles are filled from within Kibagare. Value stays local. Skills are built locally. The centre grows the capacity of its own people — not outside consultants.
🌍 Designed to scale
The model is replicable. Once proven in Kibagare, this framework can be adapted and implemented across other underserved communities in Kenya and beyond.
